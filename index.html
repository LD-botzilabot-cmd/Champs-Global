Why Check All Three Bureaus?

Lenders may pull your credit from any of the three bureaus. Each bureau may have slightly different information, so checking all three ensures you see what lenders see. You're entitled to one free report per year from each bureau.

Why Credit Reports Matter

Your credit report is a detailed record of your borrowing and repayment history. Lenders use this information to assess the risk of lending to you and determine your eligibility for loans, credit lines, and favorable interest rates.

Regularly checking your credit reports helps you:

✓ Catch errors that could hurt your credit score
✓ Detect signs of identity theft early
✓ Understand what lenders see when reviewing your application
✓ Improve your chances of loan approval
💡 Good news: You're entitled to a free credit report from each of the three major bureaus (TransUnion, Experian, and Equifax) once per year.

What Affects Your Credit Score

Your credit score is calculated based on five main factors. Understanding these can help you make informed decisions to improve your creditworthiness:

Payment History 35%

On-time payments vs. late or missed payments

Credit Utilization 30%

How much credit you're using vs. your total available credit

Credit History Length 15%

How long you've had credit accounts

New Credit 10%

Recent credit inquiries and new accounts

Credit Mix 10%

Variety of credit types (credit cards, loans, mortgages)

How Lenders Use Credit Reports

When you apply for funding, lenders review your credit report to assess risk and determine loan terms. Here's what they look for:

1
Minimum Score Requirements

Most business lenders prefer a credit score of 720+, though options exist for lower scores with adjusted terms.

2
Recent Payment History

Lenders pay close attention to your payment behavior over the last 12-24 months. Recent positive history can outweigh older issues.

3
Credit Inquiries

Multiple hard inquiries in a short period can temporarily lower your score. However, checking your own credit (soft inquiry) doesn't affect your score.

4
Debt-to-Income Ratio

Lenders compare your existing debt obligations to your income to ensure you can handle additional payments.

💡 Pro Tip: Before applying for funding, review all three credit reports and dispute any errors. This can improve your approval chances and help you secure better terms.
